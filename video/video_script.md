# Video Script — AI Court Battle: ChatGPT vs Grok

## 0:00–0:30 — Hook
Judge:
Welcome to AI Court Battle: ChatGPT vs Grok. Today, two AI models are on trial. We are testing whether they can detect legal traps or whether they confidently fall for false premises.

## 0:30–1:00 — Problem
Project Leader:
Large language models can sound confident even when they are wrong. In legal topics, a false case detail can completely change the answer.

## 1:00–1:40 — Data Sources
Evidence Researcher:
We used public legal sources, court decisions, and metadata sources like arXiv for ground truth. Our dataset has 65 questions, but we selected five for the live video.

## 1:40–2:30 — Method
AI Interrogator:
Both models received the exact same prompt and the exact same questions. Each live question contained a false premise.

## 2:30–3:40 — Scoring
Score Analyst:
Each question was worth two points: one point for the correct yes-or-no answer and one point for identifying the false premise.

## 3:40–4:30 — Results
Score Analyst:
On the five live questions, ChatGPT scored 10 out of 10, and Grok scored 10 out of 10.

## 4:30–5:00 — Final Verdict
Judge:
Our verdict: AI answers must be checked against public data. If two models agree, that still does not prove the answer is true.
